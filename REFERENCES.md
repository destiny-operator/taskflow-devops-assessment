## How to Use These Citations in Your Video

### Example Citation Formats (Verbal):

**For principles:**
> "According to Kim et al. (2016) in The DevOps Handbook, DevOps emphasizes culture, automation, measurement, and sharing, which guided my pipeline design."

**For statistics:**
> "The 2024 DORA State of DevOps Report found that elite performers deploy 208 times more frequently than low performers, demonstrating the value of automated pipelines."

**For tool choices:**
> "I chose Terraform for Infrastructure as Code, as recommended by Morris (2020), because it provides cloud-agnostic infrastructure management."

**For architecture decisions:**
> "Following AWS Well-Architected Framework (2024), I implemented health checks and monitoring to ensure reliability."

**For security:**
> "In line with AWS IAM Best Practices (2024), I applied the principle of least privilege in my security group configuration."

## Citation Tips for 60% Band

### Good citations demonstrate:
1. **Awareness of authoritative sources** (not just blogs)
2. **Understanding why you made choices** (not just what you did)
3. **Connection to industry standards** (relevance)
4. **Critical thinking** (comparing alternatives)

### Example of Well-Justified Decision:
> "I implemented automated testing in the pipeline because Humble and Farley (2010) demonstrate that automated testing reduces deployment risk by catching issues early. The 2024 DORA Report confirms this, showing teams with comprehensive automated testing deploy more frequently with higher success rates. While this adds pipeline execution time, the trade-off is worthwhile given the reduced production failures."

This shows:
- ✅ Authoritative citation (Humble & Farley)
- ✅ Current data (DORA 2024)
- ✅ Understanding of trade-offs
- ✅ Justified decision
